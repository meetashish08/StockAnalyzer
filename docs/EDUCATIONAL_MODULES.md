## Module 1: Stock Market Fundamentals

### Icon
📈

### Topics Covered

#### 1.1 What is a Stock/Share?
- **Definition**: Ownership in a company
- **Example**: Owning 1,000 shares of TCS = 0.0000274% ownership
- **Rights**: Profit sharing, voting rights (shareholder meetings)
- **Risk**: Value can go up or down

#### 1.2 Stock Exchanges
**Indian Exchanges**:
- NSE (National Stock Exchange)
- BSE (Bombay Stock Exchange)
- Trading Hours: 9:15 AM - 3:30 PM IST

**US Exchanges**:
- NYSE (New York Stock Exchange)
- NASDAQ (Tech-heavy exchange)
- Trading Hours: 9:30 AM - 4:00 PM ET (7:00 PM - 1:30 AM IST)

#### 1.3 How Stock Prices Are Determined
- Supply and Demand mechanism
- **Positive Factors** (Price ⬆️):
  - Strong earnings report
  - New product launch success
  - Industry tailwinds
  - Positive analyst upgrades
- **Negative Factors** (Price ⬇️):
  - Missed earnings expectations
  - Regulatory issues
  - Management scandals
  - Economic recession

#### 1.4 Market Capitalization
**Formula**: Market Cap = Stock Price × Total Shares Outstanding

| Category | Market Cap | Risk Profile | Examples |
|----------|-----------|--------------|----------|
| Large Cap | > ₹20,000 Cr | Low risk, stable | TCS, Reliance, HDFC Bank |
| Mid Cap | ₹5,000 - ₹20,000 Cr | Medium risk, moderate growth | Dixon, Polycab |
| Small Cap | < ₹5,000 Cr | High risk, high growth | Varun Beverages |

#### 1.5 Bull vs Bear Markets
**Bull Market** 🐂:
- Prices rising (up 20%+ from lows)
- Investor optimism
- Economic growth
- Good time to invest

**Bear Market** 🐻:
- Prices falling (down 20%+ from highs)
- Investor pessimism
- Economic downturn
- Opportunity for long-term buyers (buy low)

#### 1.6 Dividends Explained
- **Definition**: Portion of company profits paid to shareholders
- **Frequency**: Quarterly (US), Annually (India)
- **Example**: HDFC Bank
  - Annual Dividend: ₹19 per share
  - Stock Price: ₹1,650
  - Dividend Yield: (19/1,650) × 100 = 1.15%
  - If you own 100 shares: ₹1,900/year income

#### 1.7 Stock Splits
- **Purpose**: Make stock more affordable for retail investors
- **Example**: 2-for-1 Split
  - Before: 10 shares @ ₹2,000 = ₹20,000 total value
  - After: 20 shares @ ₹1,000 = ₹20,000 total value
  - Total value unchanged, just more shares at lower price

### Learning Outcomes
After completing this module, you will:
- Understand what stocks represent
- Know how exchanges work
- Identify market cap categories
- Distinguish bull from bear markets
- Calculate dividend yield
- Understand stock split mechanics

### Key Takeaways
1. Stocks = Ownership in companies
2. Price determined by supply/demand
3. Large cap = stable, Small cap = risky
4. Dividends = passive income
5. Stock splits = no change in value

## Module 2: Financial Metrics & Calculations

### Icon
🧮

### Topics Covered

#### 2.1 P/E Ratio (Price-to-Earnings)
**Formula**: P/E = Stock Price / Earnings Per Share (EPS)

**Interpretation**:
- **Low P/E (<15)**: Potentially undervalued or slow-growth company
- **Medium P/E (15-25)**: Fairly valued, stable growth
- **High P/E (>25)**: Growth stock or potentially overvalued

**Interactive Calculator**: Enter price and EPS to get instant P/E ratio

**Important**: Always compare P/E with industry peers. Tech companies typically have higher P/E than banks.

**Example**:
- Stock Price: ₹3,500
- EPS: ₹140
- P/E = 3,500/140 = 25.0 (fairly valued for growth stock)

#### 2.2 ROE (Return on Equity)
**Formula**: ROE = (Net Income / Shareholders' Equity) × 100

Measures how efficiently a company generates profit from equity.

| ROE Range | Rating |
|-----------|--------|
| < 10% | Poor |
| 15-20% | Good |
| > 20% | Excellent |

**Example**: Asian Paints
- Net Income: ₹3,000 Cr
- Shareholders' Equity: ₹12,000 Cr
- ROE = (3,000/12,000) × 100 = 25% ✅ Excellent

#### 2.3 P/B Ratio (Price-to-Book)
**Formula**: P/B = Stock Price / Book Value Per Share

**Interpretation**:
- P/B < 1: Trading below book value (value stock or distressed)
- P/B = 1-3: Fairly valued
- P/B > 3: Premium valuation (growth/brand value)

#### 2.4 EPS (Earnings Per Share)
**Formula**: EPS = Net Income / Total Shares Outstanding

Higher EPS = More profitable company

**Growth Analysis Example**:
| Year | EPS | Growth |
|------|-----|--------|
| 2022 | ₹100 | - |
| 2023 | ₹115 | +15% |
| 2024 | ₹132 | +14.8% |

✅ Consistent EPS growth indicates strong business performance

#### 2.5 Dividend Yield
**Formula**: Dividend Yield = (Annual Dividend / Stock Price) × 100

**Interactive Calculator**: Enter dividend and price to get yield%

**Typical Ranges**:
- < 2%: Growth companies (reinvest profits)
- 2-4%: Balanced companies
- > 4%: Dividend-focused (utilities, REITs)
- > 7%: ⚠️ Too high - may be unsustainable

#### 2.6 Debt-to-Equity Ratio
**Formula**: D/E = Total Debt / Shareholders' Equity

| D/E Range | Risk Level |
|-----------|-----------|
| < 0.5 | Low Risk (Conservative) |
| 0.5 - 1.0 | Moderate (Balanced) |
| > 1.0 | High Risk (Leveraged) |

#### 2.7 CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate)
**Formula**: CAGR = [(Ending Value / Beginning Value)^(1/Years)] - 1

**Interactive Calculator**: Enter start value, end value, and years

**Benchmark Returns**:
- 8-10%: Conservative/Fixed Income
- 12-15%: Good equity returns
- 18%+: Excellent performance

**Example**:
- Starting Value: ₹1,00,000
- Ending Value: ₹2,00,000
- Years: 5
- CAGR = [(2,00,000/1,00,000)^(1/5)] - 1 = 14.87%

### Learning Outcomes
After completing this module, you will:
- Calculate and interpret P/E, ROE, P/B ratios
- Assess company profitability using EPS
- Evaluate dividend stocks using yield
- Analyze financial leverage via D/E ratio
- Calculate investment returns using CAGR
- Use interactive calculators for quick analysis

### Key Takeaways
1. P/E < 15 = Value, P/E > 25 = Growth
2. ROE > 20% = Efficient company
3. EPS growth = Business strength
4. D/E > 1 = High debt risk
5. CAGR = Smoothed annual return

## Module 3: Technical Analysis Basics

### Icon
📊

### Topics Covered

#### 3.1 Moving Averages (MA)
**Definition**: Average of closing prices over N days

**Formula**: SMA = (Sum of closing prices over N days) / N

**50-Day DMA**:
- Short to medium-term trend
- Price above 50 DMA = Bullish
- Price below 50 DMA = Bearish
- Used by active traders

**200-Day DMA**:
- Long-term trend indicator
- Price above 200 DMA = Strong uptrend
- Price below 200 DMA = Weak/downtrend
- Most watched by institutions

#### 3.2 Golden Cross & Death Cross
**Golden Cross** ✨:
- 50 DMA crosses **above** 200 DMA
- **Signal**: Strong bullish indicator
- **Action**: Consider buying
- **Historical Accuracy**: 65-70%

**Death Cross** 💀:
- 50 DMA crosses **below** 200 DMA
- **Signal**: Strong bearish indicator
- **Action**: Consider selling/hedging
- **Historical Accuracy**: 60-65%

#### 3.3 RSI (Relative Strength Index)
**Formula**: RSI = 100 - [100 / (1 + RS)]
- Where RS = Average Gain / Average Loss over 14 days

**Scale**: 0-100

| RSI Range | Signal |
|-----------|--------|
| < 30 | Oversold (Buy signal) |
| 30-70 | Neutral (Hold) |
| > 70 | Overbought (Sell signal) |

**Visual**: Gradient bar from red (0) → yellow (50) → green (100)

#### 3.4 MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)
**How to Read**:
- MACD Line crosses **above** Signal Line → Bullish (buy signal)
- MACD Line crosses **below** Signal Line → Bearish (sell signal)
- Histogram expanding → Trend strengthening
- Histogram shrinking → Trend weakening

#### 3.5 Support & Resistance
**Support Level** 🛡️:
- Price level where buying interest prevents further decline
- Think of it as a "floor" where buyers step in

**Resistance Level** 🔝:
- Price level where selling pressure prevents further rise
- Think of it as a "ceiling" where sellers emerge

**Key Rule**: When support breaks → becomes new resistance
When resistance breaks → becomes new support

#### 3.6 Volume Analysis
**Volume** = Number of shares traded

**Interpretation**:
- ✅ Price ⬆️ + High Volume = Strong uptrend (confident buyers)
- ⚠️ Price ⬆️ + Low Volume = Weak rally (may reverse)
- ✅ Price ⬇️ + High Volume = Strong downtrend (panic selling)
- ⚠️ Price ⬇️ + Low Volume = Weak decline (may rebound)

### Learning Outcomes
After completing this module, you will:
- Calculate and interpret moving averages
- Identify Golden Cross and Death Cross patterns
- Use RSI to spot overbought/oversold conditions
- Read MACD indicators for trend signals
- Identify support and resistance levels
- Confirm trends using volume analysis

### Key Takeaways
1. Price > 200 DMA = Long-term uptrend
2. Golden Cross = Bullish, Death Cross = Bearish
3. RSI < 30 = Oversold buy opportunity
4. High volume confirms price moves
5. Support breaks = New resistance

## Module 4: Criteria for Good Stocks

### Icon
✅

### Topics Covered

#### 4.1 Universal Quality Checklist
7 Must-Have Criteria:
1. ✓ Consistent revenue growth (>10% YoY)
2. ✓ Strong profit margins (>15%)
3. ✓ Low debt-to-equity (<0.5 for growth)
4. ✓ Good ROE (>15%)
5. ✓ Competitive moat (brand/patents/network)
6. ✓ Strong management track record
7. ✓ Growing market opportunity

#### 4.2 Stock Categories & Criteria

**Value Stocks** 💎:
- Low P/E (<15)
- Low P/B (<3)
- High dividend yield (>3%)
- Trading below intrinsic value
- **Examples**: Banks (HDFC, ICICI), Utilities (NTPC, Power Grid)
- **Strategy**: Buy when undervalued, hold 3-5 years

**Growth Stocks** 🚀:
- Revenue growth >20% YoY
- High ROE (>20%)
- Expanding profit margins
- Large addressable market
- **Examples**: Tech (TCS, Infosys), New-age (Zomato, Nykaa)
- **Note**: P/E may be high (25-40) but justified by growth

**Dividend Stocks** 💰:
- 10+ year dividend history
- Dividend yield >3%
- Payout ratio <60%
- Strong cash flow
- **Examples**: ITC, Coal India, ONGC
- **Best for**: Retirees and income-focused investors

#### 4.3 Red Flags to Avoid
🚩 Warning Signs:
- ❌ Declining revenue for 2+ consecutive quarters
- ❌ Debt-to-equity > 2 (highly leveraged)
- ❌ Negative cash flow from operations
- ❌ Frequent management changes
- ❌ Corporate governance issues
- ❌ Pledged promoter shares > 50%
- ❌ Consistent delays in financial reporting
- ❌ Accounting irregularities or restatements
- ❌ Loss of key customers (> 25% revenue)
- ❌ Regulatory investigations or penalties

#### 4.4 Competitive Moat Analysis
**Moat** = Sustainable competitive advantage that protects profits

**6 Types of Moats**:
1. 🏰 **Brand Power**: Strong brand commands premium pricing (Asian Paints, Nestle)
2. 🔒 **Patents/IP**: Intellectual property creates barriers (Pharma, Tech)
3. 🌐 **Network Effects**: Value increases with more users (PayTM, Zomato)
4. 💰 **Cost Leadership**: Lowest cost producer (Reliance, Amazon)
5. 🔄 **Switching Costs**: High cost to change providers (Enterprise software, Banks)
6. 📜 **Regulatory License**: Government approval required (Telecom, Insurance)

### Learning Outcomes
After completing this module, you will:
- Apply 7-point quality checklist to stocks
- Distinguish value, growth, and dividend stocks
- Identify red flags before investing
- Assess competitive moat strength
- Avoid common investment mistakes
- Build a watchlist of quality stocks

### Key Takeaways
1. Quality > Price - Don't buy junk cheap
2. Moat protects profits long-term
3. Avoid heavily leveraged companies (D/E > 2)
4. Value stocks = Low P/E + dividend
5. Growth stocks = High growth + ROE

## Module 5: Portfolio Management

### Icon
💼

### Topics Covered

#### 5.1 Professional Investment Process
**6-Step Framework**:

1. **Top-Down Analysis**:
   - Analyze macroeconomic trends (GDP, inflation, interest rates)
   - Identify growth sectors vs declining sectors
   - Sector rotation strategy based on economic cycle

2. **Stock Screening**:
   - Apply quantitative filters (P/E, ROE, market cap)
   - Liquidity requirements (avg volume > 100K shares/day)
   - Create shortlist of 50-100 stocks from 5,000+

3. **Fundamental Analysis**:
   - Read annual reports (10-K, 10-Q, AR)
   - Management quality assessment
   - Competitive position analysis (Porter's 5 Forces)
   - Financial statement deep dive (3-5 years)
   - DCF valuation and price target

4. **Risk Assessment**:
   - Concentration limits (max 5-10% per stock)
   - Sector diversification (<25% per sector)
   - Beta analysis (volatility vs market)
   - Stress testing portfolio

5. **Position Sizing**:
   - High conviction: 5-8% allocation
   - Medium conviction: 2-4% allocation
   - Low conviction: 0.5-1% allocation
   - Total: 15-30 stocks optimal

6. **Exit Strategy**:
   - Stop loss: -15% to -20%
   - Target price achievement (sell 50%, hold rest)
   - Fundamental deterioration
   - Better opportunity elsewhere

#### 5.2 Famous Investment Strategies

**Warren Buffett - Value Investing**:
- Buy wonderful companies at fair prices
- Economic moat is essential
- Hold forever (long-term)
- Circle of competence
- **Key**: Intrinsic value > Market price

**Peter Lynch - GARP (Growth at Reasonable Price)**:
- Buy what you know
- Look for 10-baggers
- PEG ratio < 1 (PE/Growth rate)
- Visit stores, use products
- **Key**: P/E should equal growth rate

**Benjamin Graham - Deep Value**:
- Margin of safety (buy at 2/3 intrinsic value)
- Focus on balance sheet
- Ignore market emotions
- Diversify (20-30 stocks)
- **Key**: Price < Book Value

**Cathie Wood - Disruptive Innovation**:
- Focus on innovation (AI, genomics, blockchain)
- High-growth companies
- 5-year investment horizon
- Accept volatility for returns
- **Key**: Revolutionary technology

#### 5.3 Diversification Guidelines

**Optimal Portfolio Mix**:
- Number of stocks: 15-30
- Sectors: 5-7 different
- Max per stock: 8-10%
- Max per sector: 25%

**Market Cap Allocation**:
- Large Cap: 60% (stable core)
- Mid Cap: 25% (growth potential)
- Small Cap: 15% (high risk/reward)

**Important Rule**: Never put more than 10% in a single stock, no matter how confident!

#### 5.4 Rebalancing Strategy
**When to Rebalance**:
- ✅ Quarterly or Semi-annually: If any stock > 12% (trim to 8-10%)
- ✅ After major market moves: If sector concentration > 30%
- ✅ When fundamentals change: Deteriorating business quality
- ✅ Tax-loss harvesting: Sell losers in March for tax benefit

### Learning Outcomes
After completing this module, you will:
- Follow a professional 6-step investment process
- Choose an investment strategy matching your style
- Diversify correctly across stocks and sectors
- Size positions based on conviction level
- Know when to rebalance portfolio
- Set clear entry and exit rules

### Key Takeaways
1. 15-30 stocks is optimal diversification
2. Max 10% per stock, 25% per sector
3. Buffett = Value, Lynch = GARP, Graham = Deep Value
4. Rebalance when stock > 12% of portfolio
5. 60% Large, 25% Mid, 15% Small cap

## Module 6: Success Criteria & Benchmarks

### Icon
🎯

### Topics Covered

#### 6.1 Portfolio Performance Metrics

**Absolute Returns**:
- Formula: [(Ending Value - Beginning Value) / Beginning Value] × 100
- Interpretation: Total gain/loss percentage
- **Good**: >12% annually

**CAGR**:
- Formula: [(Ending Value / Beginning Value)^(1/years)] - 1
- Interpretation: Smoothed annual growth rate
- **Good**: 12-15% long-term

**Alpha**:
- Formula: Portfolio Return - Benchmark Return
- Interpretation: Excess return vs market
- **Good**: +2% to +5%

**Beta**:
- Formula: Covariance(Portfolio, Market) / Variance(Market)
- Interpretation: Volatility vs market (1.0 = market risk)
- **Good**: 0.8-1.2 (similar to market)

**Sharpe Ratio**:
- Formula: (Portfolio Return - Risk-free Rate) / Std Deviation
- Interpretation: Risk-adjusted returns
- **Good**: >1 is good, >2 is excellent

**Max Drawdown**:
- Formula: (Trough Value - Peak Value) / Peak Value
- Interpretation: Largest peak-to-trough decline
- **Good**: <25% (low risk)

#### 6.2 Benchmark Comparisons

**Indian Benchmarks** (10-year historical CAGR):
- Nifty 50: ~12%
- Sensex: ~11%
- Nifty Midcap 100: ~15%
- Nifty Smallcap 100: ~17%

**US Benchmarks** (10-year historical CAGR):
- S&P 500: ~10%
- NASDAQ: ~12%
- Dow Jones: ~8%
- Russell 2000: ~9%

**Goal**: Beat benchmark by 2-5% annually (after fees)

#### 6.3 Success by Investor Type

**Conservative Investor**:
- Target CAGR: 8-10%
- Max Drawdown: <15%
- Allocation: 60% Large Cap, 30% Debt, 10% Mid/Small
- Time Horizon: 3-5 years

**Moderate Investor**:
- Target CAGR: 12-15%
- Max Drawdown: 15-25%
- Allocation: 60% Large, 25% Mid, 15% Small
- Time Horizon: 5-7 years

**Aggressive Investor**:
- Target CAGR: 18%+
- Max Drawdown: 30%+ acceptable
- Allocation: 40% Large, 35% Mid, 25% Small
- Time Horizon: 7-10+ years

#### 6.4 Time Horizon Success Criteria

| Period | Goal | Multiplier | Beat |
|--------|------|-----------|------|
| 1 year | > 7% | 1.07x | Inflation |
| 3 years | > 9% | 1.30x | FD returns |
| 5 years | > 14.4% | 2.0x | Double money |
| 7 years | > 15% | 2.66x | Market avg |
| 10 years | > 12-15% | 3.0-4.0x | Wealth creation |

**Rule of 72**: Years to double = 72 / Annual Return %
- Example: At 12% CAGR, money doubles in 72/12 = 6 years

#### 6.5 Success Checklist
✅ Are You Succeeding?
- [ ] Beating benchmark by 2-5% annually
- [ ] CAGR > 12% over 5+ years
- [ ] Maximum drawdown < 30%
- [ ] Win rate > 60% (60% stocks profitable)
- [ ] Average gain > Average loss (2:1 ratio ideal)
- [ ] Portfolio growing faster than inflation
- [ ] Diversified (15-30 stocks, 5+ sectors)
- [ ] Regular rebalancing (every 6-12 months)
- [ ] Sticking to investment plan (not panic selling)
- [ ] Learning from mistakes (maintaining journal)

### Learning Outcomes
After completing this module, you will:
- Calculate key performance metrics (Alpha, Beta, Sharpe)
- Compare your returns against benchmarks
- Set realistic goals based on investor type
- Use Rule of 72 for wealth planning
- Assess portfolio success using checklist
- Understand time horizon impact on returns

### Key Takeaways
1. 12-15% CAGR is good long-term target
2. Beat benchmark by 2-5% = Success
3. Sharpe > 1 = Good risk-adjusted returns
4. Rule of 72: Double money in 6 years at 12%
5. 60% win rate is acceptable for equities
